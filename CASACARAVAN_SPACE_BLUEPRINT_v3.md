## The Three Zones

### House — Known
Warm, functional, reliable. Users come here to do something.
Tools, studios, labs. Every room has a purpose.
This is where revenue lives — free access, one-time purchases, membership.

### Garden — Semi-known
Alive and changing. Seasonal light, growing things.
The idea basket lives here. Community seeds are planted here.
Users come here to leave something or find something unexpected.

### Forest — Unknown
No map. Surprises. Games. Generative art.
Things that appear and disappear.
This is what makes people come back.
"Last time there was something here — what's there now?"

## Who Lives Here

**18–25:** Enters through the forest. Stays for the house.
Wants to create, share, belong. Short attention, deep hunger.
Needs: shareable outputs, community feeling, discovery.

**25–35:** Enters through the garden. Comes back for the house.
Wants meaning alongside productivity. Building something.
Needs: tools that work, knowledge that sticks, routine.

**35+:** Enters through the house. Sometimes wanders to the garden.
Wants depth, quiet, quality. No noise.
Needs: real information, no gimmicks, trust.

## The Forest — Design Principles

- No fixed map. Users discover by wandering.
- Things change. Something that was there last week may be gone.
- Surprises are real — not fake "easter eggs" but genuinely unexpected.
- Games are short (under 5 minutes) and philosophical.
- Generative experiences respond to the user — time of day, season, mood.
- The forest remembers nothing. Each visit is new.
